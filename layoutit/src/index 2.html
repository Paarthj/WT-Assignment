SAHAKARNAGAR ASSOCIATION

Sahakara Nagar is a residential suburb near Bangalore , India . It is situated on the outskirts of Bangalore. Sahakanagar is formed by Ministry of Communication Employees housing society and has developed to this extent under the efforts of Sahakaranagar Residents welfare association. Ever since the inclusion of 45 new wards to Bangalore city in 2008, Sahakara Nagar comes under the BBMP (Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike) limits. The locality is accessible via NH 7 on the East. Sahakara Nagar was formed in the early 1980s as a co-operative society for Telecom and Post & Telegraph employees of the Government of India. It has over 8 well maintained parks and two Playgrounds maintained by BBMP.
Sahakarnagar Welfare Association is an association of the residents of sahakarmagar who come together to form a community ot get all the residents together and organise various events for them and also towards the welfare of the locality. The memners of the association are elected by the residents once in 3 years and anyone can contest the elections.

Neighbourhood:

Sahakara Nagar comes in parts, under both Byatarayanapura and Kodigehalli wards of the BBMP, and is flanked by Hebbal, Thindlu, Vidyaranyapura, and Kempapura. However, Sahakara Nagar is located outside the bounds of Bangalore City.

Acivities:

Some of the activities of Sahakarnagar are:
1. Sahakarnagar Habba - It is an yearly festival celebrated where all the residents of Sahakarnagar come together to share joy and happiness. It is a 2days event which is usually in the month of November. Various activities are organised for the residents which helps the residents to know each other and bring them together. Activities are of a wide range for kids as well as adults of all age groups. During this time, there are various food trucks on the streets as well. Cultural events are organised in the event where differnt institutes come up to display their art forms and talent. It is a mini fair to which all the residents await eagerly year after year.



2. Cycle Day - As the names says it, it is an open cycle day which is organised once in 2 months. This is usually conducted on a weekend where all the roads are blocked for vehicles and only cycles can be seen on the road. Those who have an cycle use their and those who dont have can borrow from the local cycle mela organised just for this day. People of all age grouos participate enthusiasctically. Is is a step towards reducing pollution and to save the Mother Earth.



3. Yoga Day - This event is organised on June 21 which is the International Yoga Day. It has significance in various parts of the world and is celebrated globally. Sahakarnagar as a community also adds to this by stressing the importance of Yoga in life and encouraging eveyone to participate and also practice regularly. There are also various Yoga schools in this loaclity to guide the people and it is also taught in every school of Sahakarnagar.



4. Ganesha Making - During the Ganesha Chaturthi, workhops are organised to teach the residents to make mud/clay Ganesha. This is a step to encourage people to use Eco-friendly idols for the worship to reduce the use of Plaster of Paris idols. Children also take part in this event with equal interest as that of the elders. IT is one more fun day where people come together, have fun and take back a hand made idol for worship with joy.
